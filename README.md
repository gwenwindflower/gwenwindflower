### ğŸŒ¶ï¸ğŸ¤·ğŸ»â€â™€ï¸ A handful of heresies

1. Data visualization is overrated! Don't get me wrong, I love visuals, and there are many, many analyses where they help communicate in a way words can't (I'm a girl with the complete works of Tufte on my shelf) â€” but in BI, visualization is treated as the go-to, the standard, THE final destination of all this engineering and modeling effort. It's past time we remembered that this week's revenue numbers might work better in a short paragraph, and thankfully LLMs are getting us there.

2. You're looking at too much data. Your brain _cannot_ track 50 metrics hour-by-hour, day-by-day in a way that is useful and effective. Most people try to do this becaue they're afraid of being asked a question about their domain they can't answer, and the anxious need to watch all these numbers makes them _less_ sensitive to the signal, not more. It's theater. Instead, focus on a handful of metrics and your theories of their growth levers, do that consistently over a sustained period of time until you've established confidence in the right levers. Stay disciplined! Push on them, measure what happens. Map out the machine, and set up thresholds to alert you when the machine inevitably starts to change. Rinse. Repeat.
